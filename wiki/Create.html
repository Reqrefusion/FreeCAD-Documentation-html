FreeCAD – Engineering 3D Design Prompt (Academic Research):

Create a detailed and clear 3D engineering model using FreeCAD for an academic research project titled:

“Design of a Sand-Based Thermal Heat Exchanger System Using Buried Copper Pipes.”

The model should include the following elements with accurate proportions and parametric dimensions:

1. Insulated Wooden Box:

  • Rectangular box with dimensions 2 m (length) × 2 m (width) × 1 m (height).
  • Wooden outer structure with an internal insulation layer.
  • The box should be filled with sand.

2. Sand Layer:

  • Homogeneous sand volume inside the box.
  • Indicate the sand level clearly.

3. Copper Pipe Heat Exchanger:

  • Total pipe length: 30 meters.
  • Material: copper.
  • Pipes arranged in a coiled (serpentine) configuration.
  • Pipes buried at a depth of 50 cm from the top surface of the sand.
  • Pipes fully embedded inside the sand volume.

4. Hydraulic Circuit (Closed Loop):

  • Inlet and outlet copper pipes exiting the box.
  • Connection to an external water storage tank.
  • Include a circulation pump connected to form a closed-loop system.

5. Measurement Instruments:

  • Temperature sensor at the pipe inlet.
  • Temperature sensor at the pipe outlet.
  • Flow meter installed on the pipe to measure water flow rate.

6. Visualization Requirements:

  • Use simple colors to distinguish materials (wood, sand, copper, water circuit).
  • Include a sectional cut view to clearly show buried pipes inside the sand.
  • Label all main components (box, sand, pipes, pump, tank, sensors).
  • Ensure the design is clean, technical, and suitable for inclusion in a scientific research paper.

7. Units and Standards:

  • Units: meters (m).
  • Scale: realistic (1:1).
  • Purpose: scientific visualization and engineering analysis (not aesthetic rendering).
